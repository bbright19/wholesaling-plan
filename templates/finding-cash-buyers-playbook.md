# Finding Cash Buyers — Playbook

> A repeatable process for building a cash-buyers list in PG County (20748 / 20745) before you lock a deal.
> Rule #1: **build the list BEFORE you have a deal.** When you go under contract you'll have days, not weeks, to assign — a warm list is what makes that possible.
> Capture every buyer you find in `buyers-list.md` (Pipeline table + Buyer Profile for the serious ones).

---

## Tier 1 — Start here (free, targeted, highest ROI)

### 1. Mine public county records — recent cash sales (best source)
Cash buyers leave a paper trail: a recorded **deed with NO accompanying deed of trust (mortgage)** = a cash purchase. The buyer (grantee) is an active investor in your exact market.

**Step-by-step (Maryland / PG County):**
1. Go to **mdlandrec.net** (free account) for recorded land records, and **SDAT** (Maryland State Dept. of Assessments & Taxation) for ownership/assessment data.
2. Pull SOLD properties in 20748 / 20745 from the last **6–12 months**.
3. For each, check whether a **deed of trust** was recorded alongside the deed. **No mortgage = cash buyer.**
4. Note the **grantee** (buyer). Flag **repeat buyers and LLC names** — these are your A-list.
5. Find the human behind the LLC: search **OpenCorporates** (and Maryland Business Express) or a plain web search for the LLC's registered agent / owner, then **skip-trace** to a phone/email.
6. Add to `buyers-list.md`; call/text to learn their buy box.

> Repeat-buyer LLCs are gold — they've bought multiple times and will buy again.

### 2. Target "Fix & Flip" hotspots (proven active buyers)
Find homes that were **bought and then re-listed a few months later** — that's a flipper actively working your area.
1. On **Zillow / Redfin**, look for recent sales that came back on the market shortly after (short ownership window, often with a renovation bump in price).
2. Check the **property appraiser / SDAT records** to find the **LLC that bought and flipped it**.
3. Reach out — they're a proven cash buyer buying in your zip *right now*. Add to the list.

### 3. Call the "We Buy Houses" advertisers
The people advertising ARE cash buyers. Sources: bandit signs, Google "sell house fast Temple Hills / Oxon Hill," Craigslist & FB Marketplace real-estate ads.
- Script: *"Hi, I'm a wholesaler — I find off-market deals in PG County. I came across your ad. What's your buy box so I can send you deals that fit?"*

### 4. REIA meetings & local investor meetups
The DMV has active groups (search Meetup + Facebook for "DC/Maryland real estate investors," DCREIA, PG County investor groups). Cash buyers attend in person — one evening can net several relationships. Fits an evening/weekend schedule.

## Tier 2 — Scale it up

### 5. Leverage local title companies & closing attorneys
They see who's *actively closing* every week — and they want your future business, so they'll often share for free.
1. Visit (or call) a local title company / closing attorney and ask for the **business development manager**.
2. Introduce yourself as an active wholesaler/investor.
3. Ask for a list of the **top 25–50 cash buyers** who have closed deals with them in the **last 3 months**.
- Bonus: also crosses off the "find an investor-friendly title company" to-do.

### 6. Online communities
- **BiggerPockets** — forums + marketplace; post that you source PG County deals.
- **Facebook groups** — DMV investor/wholesaler groups; let buyers raise their hand.

### 7. Hard money lenders
They fund investors constantly and know who's actively buying. Ask which clients are buying in PG County.

### 8. Auctions
Auction.com, Hubzu, foreclosure / courthouse-steps sales. Attendees are almost all cash buyers — collect contacts.

### 9. Investor data tools (skip the manual grind)
Set filters to find cash buyers who **own multiple properties** in your zip code.
- **PropStream**, **PropWire**, **PropertyReach** — cash + absentee + portfolio-owner filters by area.
- **BatchLeads / DealMachine** — list building + skip tracing.
- Good fit for tech-leveraged list building.

## What to capture for each buyer (the buy box)
For every buyer, fill these in `buyers-list.md`:
- Target areas / zips
- Property type (SFR matches our focus)
- Price range
- Min beds / baths / sqft
- **Rehab appetite** (light vs. full gut) + **finish standard**
- Flip or hold (BRRRR)?
- **Rehab budget per sqft** → also feeds the buyer repair cheat sheet
- Proof of funds + how fast they close

## Fastest path this week
1. **Pull 6–12 months of cash sales** in 20748 / 20745 from county records → list repeat-buyer LLCs (skip-trace via OpenCorporates).
2. **Find 3–5 fix-&-flip LLCs** via bought-then-relisted homes on Zillow/Redfin.
3. **Call 3–5 "We Buy Houses" advertisers** and capture their buy boxes.
4. **Ask one title company's BD manager** for their top 25–50 recent cash buyers.
5. **Commit to one REIA / investor meetup.**

That can produce **5–10+ real buyers** to start the list.

## Weekly buyers-list routine (sustain it)
- Add **3–5 new buyers** per week (rotate sources above).
- Re-touch existing buyers ~**monthly** so the list stays warm.
- Keep Tier A buyers' boxes current — those are who you call first on a deal.
