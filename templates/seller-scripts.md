# Seller Call Scripts & Frameworks

> Core principle: It's about THEIR problem, not your deal. You're offering a fast, easy, certain sale to someone who values speed and convenience over top dollar.

---

## 1. The Opening (Cold Outreach)

**Phone / Text:**
> "Hi [Name], my name is [Your Name]. I'm a local real estate investor here in the DC area. I came across your property at [address] and wanted to see if you'd ever consider an offer on it? No pressure at all."

**If they own it but it's not the one you called about (probate):**
> "I'm sorry for your loss. I work with families handling estate properties and I try to make the process as simple as possible. Would now be a bad time?"

---

## 2. The 4 Discovery Questions (Qualify Motivation)

Don't pitch. Ask and listen.

1. **Condition** — "Tell me about the property. What kind of shape is it in?"
2. **Timeline** — "If you did sell, how soon would you want to be done with it?"
3. **Price** — "Do you have a number in mind that would make it worth it for you?"
4. **Motivation** — "What's got you thinking about selling now?" / "What would selling free you up to do?"

> The gold is in the WHY. Foreclosure, inherited property, tired landlord, divorce, relocation = motivated.

---

## 3. Making the Offer (Use the 70% Rule)

> Run the numbers in the Deal Calculator first. MAO = (ARV x 0.70) - Repairs - Your Fee.

**Anchor low, justify with condition:**
> "Based on what comparable homes are selling for and the work this one needs, the number that works for me is around $[MAO]. I know that's likely below what you'd get listing it with an agent — but I can close in [X] days, all cash, no repairs, no showings, no commissions. Does that work for you?"

---

## 4. Objection Handling

**"That's too low."**
> "I completely understand. My offer is built around the repairs and reselling it. The trade-off is speed and certainty — no agent fees, no repairs, no waiting. If you have more time and want top dollar, listing might be your best bet. But if a quick, clean close matters more, I'm your guy."

**"I need to think about it."**
> "Totally fair. What specifically would you want to think through? I'm happy to walk through any of it now."

**"I'm getting other offers."**
> "Smart — you should. What I bring is certainty: I won't ask for repairs, I won't back out over financing, and I can close on your timeline. Keep me in mind if the others fall through."

**"Are you an agent? Are you going to list it?"**
> "No, I'm a buyer/investor. I'm not listing it — I'm making you a direct offer to purchase. (Note: depending on your contract, you may assign it to another investor — be transparent per your state's laws.)"

---

## 5. When to Walk Away

- Seller wants full retail value (no spread for you).
- No real motivation (just "testing the market").
- Property numbers don't work even at your MAO.
- Title issues you can't resolve. (Note them, follow up later.)

> Walking away IS a skill. Most leads won't convert. That's the funnel.

---

## 6. The Follow-Up (Where the Money Is)

> Most deals close on the 5th-7th contact, not the 1st. Log every lead in the Lead Tracker and set follow-up reminders.

**Follow-up text:**
> "Hi [Name], just checking back on [address]. My offer still stands if a quick, clean sale would help. No rush — here whenever you're ready."
