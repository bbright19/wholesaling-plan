## Phase 1: Foundation (Weeks 1-2)
**Goal: Understand the game before you play it**

### Learn the core concepts:
- What wholesaling actually is (assignment vs double close)
- Key terms: ARV, MAO, assignment fee, earnest money, equitable interest
- The 70% rule: MAO = (ARV x 0.70) - Repair Costs
- How assignable contracts work
- Your state's laws (DC/MD/VA wholesale regulations)

### Resources:
- YouTube: Wholesaling Inc channel, Jamil Damji (AstroFlipping)
- Book: "If You Can't Wholesale After This" by Todd Fleming (short, practical)
- BiggerPockets forums - wholesaling section
- Listen to a few BiggerPockets podcast episodes on wholesaling

### Action item:
By end of Week 2, you should be able to explain the entire process to someone from start to finish without notes.

---

## Phase 2: Know Your Market (Weeks 3-4)
**Goal: Understand your specific area like a local expert**

### Tasks:
- Pick your target area (1-2 zip codes or neighborhoods to start)
- Study sold comps on Zillow/Redfin - what are houses selling for?
- Learn the difference between retail value vs investor price
- Drive through your target area - notice distressed properties, vacant lots, boarded windows
- Understand property types (SFH, townhomes, what investors in your area want)

### Tools to explore:
- PropStream (free trial) - pull leads, run comps, see ownership info
- County tax assessor website - public ownership records
- Privy or DealMachine - optional but helpful

### Action item:
Analyze 10 recent sales in your target area. Practice running comps and estimating ARV.

---

## Phase 3: Build Your Buyers List (Weeks 3-5, parallel)
**Goal: Know who you're selling to BEFORE you find a deal**

### How to find cash buyers:
- County records - search for recent cash purchases (no mortgage recorded)
- BiggerPockets forums - introduce yourself, connect with local investors
- Facebook groups - search "[Your City] Real Estate Investors"
- REI meetups - go to 2-3 in the DC/MD/VA area (Meetup.com)
- Craigslist/Facebook Marketplace - look for "we buy houses" ads, those are your buyers

### Action item:
Build a list of 10-20 cash buyers with their name, number, email, and what they're looking for (area, property type, price range).

## Phase 4: Lead Generation (Weeks 5-7)
**Goal: Start finding motivated sellers**

### Lead sources (start with 1-2):

| Source | Cost | Difficulty |
|--------|------|-----------|
| Driving for dollars | Free (gas only) | Easy |
| Probate records (courthouse) | Free | Medium |
| PropStream lists (absentee owners, pre-foreclosure) | ~$99/mo | Easy |
| Direct mail | $0.50-$2/piece | Medium |
| Cold calling | Free (your time) | Hard but effective |
| Bandit signs | Cheap | Easy (check local laws) |

### Recommended start:
Driving for dollars + pulling probate records. Low cost, high quality leads.

### Action item:
Generate a list of 50+ potential leads in your target area.

---

## Phase 5: Outreach & Negotiation (Weeks 7-9)
**Goal: Start talking to sellers**

### Learn:
- How to have the initial conversation (it's about THEIR problem, not your deal)
- Key questions: motivation, timeline, property condition, price expectation
- How to make an offer using the 70% rule
- How to handle objections
- When to walk away

### Scripts/frameworks:
- "Hey, I noticed you might have a property at [address]. I work with some investors in the area and wanted to see if you'd ever consider an offer..."
- Be genuine, not salesy. You're solving a problem for people who want a quick, easy sale.

### Action item:
Make contact with 20+ sellers. Expect most to say no. You need volume.

---

## Phase 6: Contract & Close (Weeks 9-12)
**Goal: Lock up your first deal**

### What you need:
- An assignable purchase agreement (get one reviewed by a local RE attorney - worth $200-300)
- Understand earnest money (typically $100-$500 for wholesale deals)
- Know your title company - find one that's investor-friendly and handles assignments
- Assignment contract - the agreement between you and your buyer

### The close process:
1. Seller agrees to your price -> sign purchase agreement
2. You find a buyer at a higher price -> sign assignment contract
3. Title company handles closing -> you collect your fee at the closing table

### Action item:
Have your contracts ready and a title company identified BEFORE you need them.

## Mindset Reminders

- Your first deal will probably take longer than 12 weeks. That's normal.
- Treat it like preseason - you're building the skills and systems. The goals come later.
- Most people quit at week 6 when they haven't made money yet. You won't because you've trained through harder things than cold calls.
- Track your numbers: leads contacted, conversations had, offers made. It's a funnel, just like anything else.

## Key Formulas

- **MAO (Maximum Allowable Offer)** = ARV x 0.70 - Repair Costs - Your Assignment Fee
- **ARV (After Repair Value)** = What the house is worth fully fixed up (based on comps)
- **Assignment Fee** = Your profit (difference between your contract price and what the buyer pays)
