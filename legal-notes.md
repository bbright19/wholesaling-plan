# Legal Notes — DC / MD / VA Wholesaling

> ⚠️ **Not legal advice.** Laws change and details matter. Confirm everything below with a licensed DC/MD/VA real estate attorney before acting. Last researched: 2026-06-08.

---

## TL;DR Strategic Takeaway

- **Primary markets: Maryland + DC** (no volume cap on wholesaling).
- **Virginia: tread carefully** — only 1 assignment per rolling 12-month period without a broker's license.
- **Get an attorney** for (a) MD-compliant disclosure language, and (b) a read on any VA plans.

## 🚨 Virginia — Volume Cap (THE BIG ONE)

- **Law:** HB 917, amended Va. Code § 54.1-2100. Effective **July 1, 2024**.
- **Rule:** 1 assignment per rolling 12-month period = no license needed. **Two or more in any 12-month window = legally requires a Virginia broker's license.**
- The 12-month window rolls from your *last* assignment date — not the calendar year.
- **Impact on us:** A "recurring monthly income" strategy blows past the 1-deal cap fast. Options in VA: get licensed, double-close instead of assigning, or keep VA volume minimal and focus elsewhere.

## Maryland — New Disclosure Requirements (Wholesaler-Friendly)

- **Law:** HB 124 / SB 160, Md. Real Prop. § 10-715. Signed May 13, 2025. Effective **October 1, 2025**.
- **Rules:**
  - Wholesale buyer must give **written disclosure to the seller** BEFORE signing the purchase contract.
  - Wholesaler must **disclose to the assignee** (end buyer) BEFORE completing the assignment.
- Legal as long as there's genuine equitable interest via a valid purchase contract and no unlicensed brokerage activity.
- **No volume cap** like Virginia — makes MD a strong primary market.
- **Action:** Contracts MUST include MD-compliant disclosure language (have attorney draft/review).

## Washington DC — Legal, Standard Caveats

- Wholesaling is legal in DC.
- Core rule (same everywhere): stay on the right side of the **unlicensed-broker line** — assign your contract / equitable interest, do NOT broker the sale of someone else's property.

## The Universal Legal Line (all 3 jurisdictions)

- **Legal:** You sign a valid purchase contract → you hold **equitable interest** → you assign that interest. You're selling YOUR contract.
- **Illegal:** Marketing/advertising someone else's property for sale without a contract = acting as an **unlicensed real estate broker**.
- Keep this distinction airtight in how you talk to sellers and how you market deals.
